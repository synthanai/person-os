## ⚙️ R.H.Y.T.H.M. Engineering

**[R]itual (The Mechanism)**
"ADR Friday" - A rigid 2-hour block every Friday morning (9:00 AM - 11:00 AM) dedicated exclusively to writing and reviewing Architecture Decision Records. 

**[H]abitat (The Environment)**
Local coffee shop away from the office. Noise-canceling headphones playing brown noise. No IDEs open. Markdown editor only.

**[Y]ield (The Capability Produced)**
Prevents verbal technical decisions from evaporating. It forces the engineering team to document *why* a decision was made, not just *how* it was implemented, saving hundreds of hours of arguments when onboarding new developers six months later.

**[T]rigger (The Activation Condition)**
Opening my laptop off-network on Friday morning. 

**[H]ardline (The Strict Boundary)**
I will decline any meeting invite during this block, even from the CTO, unless the site is physically down. This is the only time I produce high-leverage intellectual property rather than writing code.

**[M]etabolic Cost (The Energy Trade)**
Writing prose is significantly harder for me than writing code. It taxes my executive function. However, the physical documentation of the system's physics is the only way to escape the "Hero Trap."
