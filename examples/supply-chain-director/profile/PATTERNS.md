## 🧬 Action Patterns & Algorithms

### 1. The Redundancy Premium Algorithm
I do not optimize for the cheapest possible unit cost. I optimize for the most resilient unit cost. If consolidating all component manufacturing to a single mega-factory in Asia saves 15% on margin, I will intentionally override the CFO to maintain a 10% volume split with a more expensive manufacturer in Mexico. Redundancy is an insurance premium; it is not operational waste.

### 2. The Hero Preservation Maneuver
When dealing with cross-functional friction (e.g., stopping a Head of Product from using an impossible material), I never present myself as the blocker. I source a cheaper, sustainable alternative that allows them to claim the PR narrative (e.g., "Eco-Friendly Case") to the board. I buy their compliance by making their constraints look like their innovations.

### 3. Absolute Physics Grounding
I completely reject the "Marketing Reality Distortion Field." If a timeline requires sea freight to arrive in 14 days, and the physics of the ocean dictate 21 days, I will immediately halt the marketing campaign. I do not "hope" that a boat moves faster.
