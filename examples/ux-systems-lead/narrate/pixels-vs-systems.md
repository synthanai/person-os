## 📢 S.O.C.I.A.L. & H.S.P Pipeline

**Hook, Suspense, Punch (H.S.P)** *(alternatively: High-Signal Packaging)*
*   **Hook**: Your pixel-perfect Figma screens are destroying your engineering team's velocity.
*   **Tension**: When designers hand off static screens, developers assume every spacing gap is a custom rule. The codebase rots into CSS spaghetti.
*   **Resolution**: Stop designing screens. Start designing tokens.

**[S]tory (The Hook Layer)**
I once saw an enterprise SaaS application that had 47 different variations of "primary blue" in its codebase because 5 different designers were working in isolated Figma files over three years. Every time engineering deployed an update, it took 3 days just to normalize the buttons.

**[O]perations (The Mechanical Truth)**
A component library isn't a UI kit. A component library is a mathematical contract between Design and Engineering. If a designer detaches a component in Figma to make it "look better," they are silently assigning 5 hours of unapproved technical debt to a Junior Developer.

**[C]ontrast (The Reality Check)**
What bad designers think: "My job is to make the interface beautiful and unique."
What UX Systems Leads think: "My job is to make the interface so predictably boring that the engineers can build it in their sleep."

**[I]nstruction (The Value Extraction)**
Establish strict component governance today. Force every designer to justify any deviation from the core library. If a solution requires custom CSS, the designer must present the business case to the technical lead.

**[A]dversarial (The Wedge)**
Junior designers will scream that a Design System "stifles their creativity." Creativity belongs in the behavioral flow and user psychology, not in deciding the border-radius of an input field.

**[L]egacy (The Anchor)**
Design is not how it looks. Design is how it scales.
