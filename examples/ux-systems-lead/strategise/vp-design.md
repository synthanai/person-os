# Designer (UX Systems Lead): Strategic Ledger

**Timestamp**: 2026-04-17
**Framework**: STRATEGISE (G.R.O.W.T.H.)
**Domain**: Designer (UX Systems Lead)
**Target**: VP of Design (Organizational Leverage)

---

## 🏔️ G.R.O.W.T.H. Mapping

**[G]oal (The Objective Vector)**
Attain VP of Design within 36 months, migrating from managing the system's pixels to managing the organization's user-centric problem-solving engine.

**[R]eality (The Current State)**
Regarded as the ultimate "systems operator." I am highly respected by engineering, but the executive suite views me as technical infrastructure overhead, not a strategic business driver.

**[O]bstacle (The Resistance)**
The "Invisible Asset" trap. A successful Design System is invisible; it simply prevents things from breaking and makes deployments faster. Because I am preventing friction rather than creating new products, my direct impact on Revenue is hard to calculate.

**[W]aypoint (The First Milestone)**
Co-author a board-level presentation with the VP of Engineering quantifying the financial ROI of the Design System directly in terms of "Engineering Hours Saved" and "Reduced Time-to-Market."

**[T]actics (The Execution Steps)**
1. Shift 20% of my time from system maintenance to high-level User Research, establishing an authoritative voice on *what* we should build, not just *how* it looks.
2. Present at two major external design/engineering conferences this year to build external market validation.
3. Stop defending typography choices; start attaching design system metrics to churn reduction metrics.

**[H]orizon (The Long Term View)**
A UX Lead builds the visual language. A VP of Design builds the organizational structure that ensures the visual language solves genuine market tension.
