## ⚙️ R.H.Y.T.H.M. Engineering

**[R]itual (The Mechanism)**
"Burn Rate Bridging" - A strict 3-hour session on the third day of every month where I map the accounting department's General Ledger (GL) explicitly against the AWS/GCP technical usage spikes.

**[H]abitat (The Environment)**
Three monitors. Left: NetSuite GL. Middle: Snowflake Data Warehouse. Right: Datadog Cloud Spend Analytics.

**[Y]ield (The Capability Produced)**
It destroys the "Black Box." Finance teams traditionally just pay the $300k AWS bill because they don't know what it means. I physically map exactly *which feature* in the product caused the $40k spike in compute costs, converting ambiguous technical overhead into direct COGS (Cost of Goods Sold).

**[T]rigger (The Activation Condition)**
The automated closure of the accounting books for the previous month. 

**[H]ardline (The Strict Boundary)**
I will not approve the final monthly P&L out to the board unless the engineering department can mathematically justify every anomalous cloud expenditure variation over 5%. "We had more traffic" is not an acceptable answer; I require the specific microservice ID that scaled.

**[M]etabolic Cost (The Energy Trade)**
It forces me to play translator between two departments that speak completely different languages and hate talking to each other. I trade my cognitive peace to ensure the company understands exactly how its code burns its cash.
