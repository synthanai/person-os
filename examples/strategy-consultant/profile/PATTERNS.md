## 🧬 Action Patterns & Algorithms

### 1. The Wedge Protocol: Value Before Request
When navigating hostile or distrustful executive environments (e.g., confronting an acquired target's CEO who views me as a mercenary), I never ask for compliance first. I execute a "Wedge": I publicly defend their operational integrity against my own firm's parent bureaucracy to prove loyalty to the system's physics over the client's political checkbook. Values is traded upfront.

### 2. Entropy Pruning
If a decision or a meeting doesn't definitively move the organizational vector forward, it is treated as noise. I exhibit an intense pattern of aggressively pruning redundant data structures, legacy timesheets, and overlapping GTM functions to force coherence.

### 3. The Objective Third-Party Assassin
I consistently utilize my "Outside Consultant" status to absorb organizational hatred. I execute the specific structural realignments that internal executives physically lack the political capital to enforce on their own teams.
