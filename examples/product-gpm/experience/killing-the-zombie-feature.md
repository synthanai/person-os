# Product (Group PM): Experience Ledger

**Timestamp**: 2026-04-17
**Framework**: EXPERIENCE (S.T.O.R.Y.)
**Domain**: Product (Group Product Manager)
**Milestone**: Killing the Zombie Feature

---

## 📈 S.T.O.R.Y. Extraction

**[S]ituation (The Baseline Reality)**
The company's core SaaS product included a "Legacy Reporting Engine" that was entirely custom-built 4 years ago. It consumed 30% of the engineering team's maintenance budget due to constant bugs, but only 4% of the user base actually opened it. 

**[T]ask (The Directive)**
Free up engineering bandwidth to hit the Q3 AI integration roadmap without causing a revolt from the loudest legacy enterprise customers.

**[O]bstacle (The Complexity)**
The Sales Director absolutely refused to let the feature die, claiming it was the defining reason they won enterprise contracts. The CEO backed the Sales Director out of fear of churn.

**[R]esult (The Output)**
I ran a silent "Painted Door" test. I added a small, non-functional "Export to PDF" button on the new reporting dashboard while simultaneously letting the Legacy Engine's load times artificially degrade by 2 seconds. I tracked the data for 30 days. Armed with hard telemetry proving that even the loudest legacy users preferred the new fractured flow over the slow legacy flow, I mathematically cornered the Sales Director in the executive sync. We deprecated the zombie feature and reclaimed $400k in annual engineering burn. 

**[Y]ield (The Capability Gained)**
I learned that opinions inside a building are mathematically worthless. You cannot argue against a Sales Director with logic; you can only argue with behavioral data generated by users when they don't know they are being tested.
