# Product (Group PM): Strategic Ledger

**Timestamp**: 2026-04-17
**Framework**: STRATEGISE (G.R.O.W.T.H.)
**Domain**: Product (Group Product Manager)
**Target**: Director of Product (Portfolio Scope)

---

## 🏔️ G.R.O.W.T.H. Mapping

**[G]oal (The Objective Vector)**
Attain Director of Product within 24 months, shifting from "managing a single product line" to "managing a portfolio of PMs."

**[R]eality (The Current State)**
I am an elite tactician. My squad hits their sprint goals 95% of the time. But I am viewed as the master of the "delivery engine," not the architect of the overarching business strategy. 

**[O]bstacle (The Resistance)**
The "Execution Trap." When you are the best at shipping features safely, leadership keeps you exactly where you are because nobody else can execute complex roadmaps cleanly. I am too valuable in the trenches.

**[W]aypoint (The First Milestone)**
Successfully execute a multi-squad "Monetization Pivot": changing a core feature from the free tier to a paid add-on, proving that I can orchestrate cross-functional revenue strategy, not just feature delivery.

**[T]actics (The Execution Steps)**
1. Draft the pricing strategy thesis without being asked, pulling data from the finance data lake.
2. Delegate the day-to-day Grooming and Stand-up ceremonies to my Senior PM, physically removing myself from the daily execution loop.
3. Align the Product Marketing Director and the Sales VP on the pricing shift before ever presenting it to the CEO.

**[H]orizon (The Long Term View)**
A Group PM builds the product. A Director of Product builds the organizational machine that decides *which* products to build. I must transition from manipulating Jira to manipulating capital.
