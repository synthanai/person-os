## 🧭 C.O.R.E. Synthesis

**[C]alling (The Problem You Solve)**
I arbitrate organizational conflict through the physical deployment of capital (engineering hours). I transition companies from feature-factories guessing at what the market wants, into mathematical risk-mitigation engines that only build what is validated.

**[O]rigin (The Defining Moment)**
I was a Junior PM who spent 6 months building a massive "social sharing" feature that the CEO swore would 10x our growth. When it launched, 0.4% of users clicked it. The engineering team was demoralized, and the CEO blamed marketing. I realized then that a Product Manager's job is not to build what leadership asks for; their job is to physically prevent engineering from building invalidated hallucinations.

**[R]eason (The Philosophical Anchor)**
Code is the most expensive way to validate a hypothesis. If you don't know the answer before the first line of code is written, you aren't doing Product Management; you are gambling with the company's burn rate.

**[E]ndurance (Your Unique Leverage)**
The ability to absorb massive organizational hatred. Sales naturally hates me because I say "no" to their specific client requests. Engineering hates me because I ask them to build throwaway prototypes instead of elegant architectures. My endurance is the ability to survive this daily emotional friction without yielding the core product vision.
