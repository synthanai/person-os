## 🧭 C.O.R.E. Synthesis

**[C]alling (The Problem You Solve)**
I commoditize infrastructure. I transition organizations from treating servers like "pets" (hand-configured, fragile) to treating them like "cattle" (disposable, automated commodities), completely eliminating deployment terror.

**[O]rigin (The Defining Moment)**
I was a sysadmin watching a single AWS availability zone go down on Black Friday. The company lost $2M an hour because the primary database was manually configured on a single EC2 instance, and the undocumented engineer who built it had quit six months prior. I learned that manual configuration is an unacceptable business continuity risk.

**[R]eason (The Philosophical Anchor)**
Uptime is not a goal; it is a symptom of reproducible physics. If you cannot delete your entire production infrastructure and rebuild it from source code in under 15 minutes, you don't have engineering; you have hope.

**[E]ndurance (Your Unique Leverage)**
The ability to divorce emotion from systems. Software engineers fall in love with their code; I don't. I view code strictly as a payload that must be transported safely across a hostile network. My leverage is the ruthless standardization of that transport.
