# Skill: GROWTH Framework

> *"If you don't know what you are sacrificing, you do not have a strategy."*

The **GROWTH** framework forces a singular attack vector from an accumulated OS ledger. Its defining feature is the **Sacrifice Clause**: every real strategy requires explicitly naming what the user is choosing NOT to pursue. Without it, the output is aspiration, not strategy.

---

## Framework Letters

| Letter | Dimension | Question It Answers |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **G** | Goal | The specific, named outcome at the 12-month horizon. Use arrival language, not directional language. |
| **R** | Reality | A ruthlessly honest assessment of today's position. Quantify the gap. |
| **O** | Obstacle | The one structural constraint that, if dissolved, collapses the distance between Reality and Goal fastest. |
| **W** | Waypoint | The specific, observable milestone that proves the vector is working at 90 days. Binary: hit or miss. |
| **T** | Tactics | 2-3 specific, repeatable mechanisms with a frequency. Not tasks. Engines. |
| **H** | Horizon | The long-term capability shift if the vector is sustained for 3-5 years. |

---

## The Sacrifice Clause

Before persisting any GROWTH output, add a **[S]acrifice** section:
> "What am I deliberately NOT pursuing to maintain this vector?"

If the user cannot name the sacrifice, they have not made a strategic decision. They have made a wish list.

---

## The 2AM Test

The vector passes if: at 2AM when the user wakes up anxious, this strategy still makes sense as the right move given the constraints they have named.

---

## Output Specification

Each letter: 60-100 words. The Sacrifice Clause is mandatory before persist.
