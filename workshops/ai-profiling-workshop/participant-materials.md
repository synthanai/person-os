## During the Session

**The most important rule:** Give specific examples, not abstract answers.

The AI will ask questions about how you operate. Your first instinct might be to answer with a principle ("I like to understand the full picture before deciding"). What makes a profile specific is an example ("When we had to decide on [situation], I went away for a day before committing because I needed to run through the second-order effects").

The more specific your examples, the more specific your PIC.

**Other things that help:**
- "I don't know" is fine once. The AI will probe gently. See if something comes.
- If a question doesn't quite fit your situation, say so. The AI will adapt.
- The managed answer and the honest answer are different things. The honest answer is more useful.

## A Note on Honest Answers

The most common mistake in profiling conversations is the managed answer: the version you'd give in a job interview, a performance review, or a conversation where you're being assessed.

The AI is not assessing you. It's trying to understand you. The honest answer  -  "I panic first and recover well" or "I give feedback late because I'm avoiding conflict"  -  produces a profile that is useful. The managed answer produces a profile that's accurate to no one.

The more honest you are, the more specifically the AI can work with you.
