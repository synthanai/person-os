# AI Profiling Workshop  -  Facilitator Guide

## Overview

| | Details |
|-|---------|
| **Duration** | 90 minutes |
| **Audience** | Experienced working professionals (no experience ceiling) |
| **Kit Used** | Profile Kit  -  Operate GPT (in-session); Lead and Matter GPT (post-session, self-directed) |
| **Group Size** | 8-20 participants |
| **Format** | In-person or virtual |

## Purpose

Give professionals a direct experience of what AI profiling feels like, and leave them with their Operate PIC built and ready to use. Demonstrate concretely that two professionals in the same room produce completely different AI profiles  -  and that difference matters.

## Materials Needed

- Participant materials (see `participant-materials.md`)
- Access to ChatGPT Plus (participants need their own accounts)
- Employee GPT - Operate set up and working (facilitator creates one; or participants create their own before the session using the pre-work instructions)
- Projector or shared screen for demonstration

## Pre-Work (send to participants 48 hours before)

"Before the workshop, please do two things:
1. Make sure you have a ChatGPT Plus account (the workshop requires it).
2. Set up your Employee GPT - Operate using the instructions at [link to profile-kit README]. This takes 5 minutes.
3. Optional: glance at the 3×3 matrix at [link to 3x3-matrix.md] so you arrive knowing the context."

## Session Outline

### 00:00 — 00:10 | Opening: The Problem

Start with the problem, not the solution.

**Say:**
"You use AI every day. But AI treats you exactly the same as every other professional in this room. It has no idea whether you're the kind of leader who reads a room before speaking or the kind who's already made their mind up before they arrive. It doesn't know whether you decide on data or instinct. It doesn't know what you've built over your career, or what you're still trying to start.

Every time you open a new conversation with AI, it starts from zero. And because it starts from zero, it defaults. It gives you the answer for the average professional, not for you.

Today we're going to fix that. Or at least, start fixing it."

---

### 00:10 — 00:20 | The 3×3 Matrix: What We're Building

Walk through the 3×3 matrix:

|  | 📥 Intelligence | ⚙️ Execution | 📤 Delivery |
|--|----------------|-------------|------------|
| 🏢 **Operate** | How you absorb knowledge | How you decide and ship | How you communicate outcomes |
| 👁️ **Lead** | How you read people | How you influence | How you build culture |
| 🌍 **Matter** | Who you are beneath the title | How you architect your career | What you're building |

**Key point to land:**
"Today we're going to map the first row: Operate. This is how you function at the day-to-day professional level. Everything else  -  Lead and Matter  -  you'll complete on your own after today.

But Operate alone will change how AI works for you."

---

### 00:20 — 00:35 | Demonstration: Live Profiling

Facilitator runs a live profiling session with one volunteer from the room, using Employee GPT - Operate.

**Instructions:**
- Run 3-4 questions from the Operate GPT (don't rush to the full profile)
- Let the audience see the conversation on screen
- After each answer, pause and say: "Notice what the AI picked up there."
- Produce a short version of the PIC at the end of the demo

**Debrief the room:**
"What did you notice? What surprised you about how specific that instruction set is?"

---

### 00:35 — 01:05 | Participant Session (30 minutes)

Participants run their own Employee GPT - Operate sessions independently.

**Facilitator instructions:**
- Circulate the room. Listen in. Don't interrupt.
- If someone is getting generic answers, check that they're giving specific examples, not abstract ones.
- If someone finishes early, ask them to read their PIC and reflect: "What's true that surprised you? What's missing?"

**Time call at 25 minutes:** "Aim to get to the synthesis output in the next 5 minutes."

---

### 01:05 — 01:20 | Debrief: Three Questions

Bring the room back together. Ask three questions:

1. "What in your profile was immediately recognisable as true?"
2. "What surprised you  -  something you'd never quite articulated before?"
3. "Who wants to share one observation from what they produced?"

Take 3-4 voluntary shares. Don't force it.

---

### 01:20 — 01:30 | Close: What Happens Next

**Walk through the next steps:**

"You've built your Operate PIC. That's row one of nine.

Here's what to do this week:
1. **Create a ChatGPT Project** and paste your Operate PIC into the Project Instructions.
2. **Run Lead GPT** on your own  -  it takes another 20 minutes. That maps how you actually lead and influence.
3. **Run Matter GPT** last. It's the deepest one. Allow 20-25 minutes. Do it when you have space to think.

Once all three are in your Project: every conversation with that AI starts from a position of genuine understanding.

Your PIC is a living document. When you change significantly  -  a new role, a major development, a career shift  -  revisit the relevant GPT and upgrade the layer that's changed."

**Final moment:**
"Two professionals in this room completed the same Operate GPT today. Their PICs are different. That difference is the point. That difference is how AI finally starts working for you, not for the average."

---

## Facilitator Notes

- If participants resist the depth of the questions ("I don't know the answer to that"), encourage specificity: "Give me one real example from the last 6 months."
- If the AI gives a generic PIC, it's usually because the answers were too abstract. The quality of the output = the quality of the examples given.
- The Lead and Matter GPTs are not designed for a group workshop setting. They work better 1:1 or individually because of the depth of the questions. This session focuses on Operate for good reason.
- If asked whether these profiles are confidential: yes. They stay in the participant's own ChatGPT account. The facilitator sees nothing unless the participant shares their screen.
