# Person OS Voice Standard

> *"The voice is not style. It is credibility."*

This file is the editorial law for the Person OS. Any content artifact generated by this system — profile files, experience ledgers, strategy vectors, operations manuals, social content — must comply with every rule below. An artifact that fails this standard is unfinished, not a draft.

The `/audit --voice` command sweeps the repo against this file. Run it before any commit.

---

## The Core Principle

Write like a senior practitioner explaining something to a peer who already knows the domain.

Not a thought leader broadcasting inspiration. Not a content creator writing for engagement metrics. Not a language model impersonating a professional. A practitioner: direct, grounded, experiential, and uninterested in sounding impressive.

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules (Repo-Wide)

### Rule 1: No Em-Dashes

Em-dashes (`—`) are banned in all output. Use commas, colons, semicolons, or parentheses instead.

```
❌ She resigned — and took $1.8M in ARR with her.
✅ She resigned, and took $1.8M in ARR with her.

❌ The system works — but only under normal conditions.
✅ The system works, but only under normal conditions.
```

### Rule 2: No AI Markers

The following phrases signal AI-generated content and are banned everywhere. Delete them on sight.

| Banned Phrase | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "results-driven" | Vague signal. Name the specific result instead. |
| "passionate about" | Not verifiable. Describe the actual behavior instead. |
| "proven track record" | Generic. Cite the specific proof. |
| "leverage" (as a verb) | Corporate cargo cult. Name what is being applied and to what. |
| "robust" | Meaningless without a failure mode to be robust against. |
| "seamless" | Name the specific friction that was eliminated. |
| "transformative" | Name the specific before/after delta. |
| "unlock" | Name what was previously inaccessible and is now accessible. |
| "in today's fast-paced world" | Delete entirely. No replacement needed. |
| "not many people talk about this" | Delete entirely. |
| "let that sink in" | Delete entirely. |
| "here is the thing" | Delete entirely. |
| "game changer" | Name the specific change and who it changes for. |
| "paradigm shift" | Describe the actual shift in concrete terms. |
| "delve into" | Use "examine," "explore," or just say what you're doing. |
| "let's dive in" | Start the content instead. |
| "this changed my life" | Name what changed and how. |

### Rule 3: No Hollow Hooks

Do NOT start any content with:
- "I" (as the first word of a Hook)
- A rhetorical question that could apply to anyone
- A vague observation about the industry

```
❌ "Are you feeling replaceable in the age of AI?"
❌ "The landscape of professional work is changing rapidly."
❌ "I have spent 20 years in strategy consulting."
✅ "You just closed a $47M acquisition. 29% of your new team has updated their LinkedIn profiles."
✅ "The most expensive sentence in any M&A deal is this one: 'The cultures are compatible.'"
```

### Rule 4: Claims Must Be Traceable

Every factual claim in a content output must trace to a specific artifact in the OS ledger:
- Numbers come from `experience/*.md` [Result]
- Mechanisms come from `operate/*.md` [Ritual] or `experience/*.md` [Yield]
- Philosophical positions come from `profile/CORE.md` [Reason] or [Calling]
- Forward direction comes from `strategise/*.md` [Horizon]

An untraced claim is a hallucination and must be removed or grounded.

### Rule 5: Voice Must Pass the Practitioner Test

Before finalizing any artifact, apply this test silently:
> "Does this sound like a senior engineer or senior operator explaining something to a peer? Or does it sound like content written to perform credibility?"

If the latter: rewrite. Specific markers of the failure state:
- Symmetrical sentence structures ("Not X. But Y. Not A. But B.")
- Rhetorical lists of 3 ("Speed. Clarity. Impact.")
- Sentences that end with a crescendo instead of a conclusion
- Any sentence that could be lifted from a motivational poster

### Rule 6: Sentence Length

- Prefer short and medium sentences.
- Occasional long sentences are fine when they serve a specific logical structure (a list of conditions, a multi-part claim).
- Avoid sentence stacking: three consecutive complex sentences require a short sentence to punctuate.

---

## The Three Tests (Quality Gate)

Every content artifact must pass all three before it is considered complete:

| Test | Question | Action If Failing |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| **ALOUD** | Would this sound flat or rehearsed if spoken aloud? | Rewrite until it sounds natural in speech. |
| **DELETE** | Can any sentence be removed without losing meaning? | Remove it. |
| **STRANGER** | Would someone with zero context understand the person's worldview, capability, and trajectory? | Add specificity. |

## What Good Looks Like

Draw from the `examples/` library. These are the benchmark:

**From Strategy Consultant:**
> "The most expensive sentence in any M&A deal is this one: 'The cultures are compatible.' It appears in every integration plan I have seen. It is supported by evidence in approximately zero of them."

**From Staff Engineer:**
> "I spent 14 months and $2.4M rewriting a system that was already working. Then we rolled it back and did it the boring way I had originally proposed. Zero production incidents in three years."

**From Scale-Up COO:**
> "She found out about the CEO transition from the company-wide announcement. Forty minutes later she resigned. In the following 90 days, $1.8M in managed ARR walked out with her."

These work because they are specific, traceable, and written by someone who was there.
